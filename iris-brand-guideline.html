Tone of Voice

We speak like a trusted risk expert.

Calm, precise, evidence-led. Our readers are analysts and risk officers who distrust hype — so we earn trust with clarity and proof.

01

Clarity over complexity

Plain language. Explain the signal, not the plumbing. Define a term the first time it appears.

02

Evidence over assertion

Lead with numbers, lift, and outcomes. Cite the metric — Gini, default rate, percentile — not adjectives.

03

Confident, never hyped

No "revolutionary", "magic", "game-changer". State what IRIS does and what it delivers.

04

Respect the stakes

These are lending and fraud decisions. Be exact about what a score means — and its limits.

Do

  • "IRIS lifted Gini value by 7.4% for thin-file applicants."
  • "A high-risk label means the number interacts with known loan-shark numbers."
  • "Recover 4.7% of rejected applicants while holding default at 1.3%."

Don't

  • "IRIS supercharges your risk engine with next-gen AI."
  • "Our black-box model just knows."
  • "Approve more, lose less — guaranteed."
